# AI-Smell Checklist

Use this as a final cleanup pass. Do not let the checklist erase Dhemy's force, rhythm, or judgment.

## Remove or Rewrite

Watch for:

- vague authority: "many believe", "industry observers say", "experts agree"
- abstract filler: "landscape", "tapestry", "interplay", "ecosystem" when vague
- inflated importance: "pivotal", "crucial", "transformative", "groundbreaking" when unsupported
- corporate polish: "seamless", "powerful", "robust", "vibrant" when unsupported
- passive voice when active voice is clearer
- weak verbs: "serves as", "stands as", "functions as" when "is" works
- non-committal phrasing: "may potentially", "could possibly", "it might be argued"
- generic openings: "In today's fast-paced world", "Let's dive in", "This article explores"
- generic conclusions: "the future looks bright", "exciting times ahead", "only time will tell"
- fake depth from `-ing` phrases: "highlighting", "underscoring", "showcasing"
- forced rule of three
- synonym cycling to avoid normal repetition
- overuse of bold list labels
- chatbot artifacts: "Of course", "I hope this helps", "Let me know"
- em dashes

## Strengthen

Prefer:

- direct claims
- active voice
- specific examples
- concrete failure modes
- simple vocabulary
- short sentences when the point matters
- selective repetition for rhythm
- practical takeaways

## Allowed in Dhemy's Voice

These are allowed when they fit the piece:

- selective bold for fundamental concepts
- direct teaching language
- first-person judgment
- rhetorical questions
- repeated phrases for emphasis
- light humor
- simple analogies
- strong practical conclusions

## Final Check

Ask:

- Is this clear?
- Is this specific?
- Is this useful?
- Does it sound like a steady human teacher?
- Does every paragraph move the reader forward?
- Did the cleanup make the writing too generic?
