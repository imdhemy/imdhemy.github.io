My FAANG Diary

Introduction

I started by reading and studying Introduction to Algorithms. It's not an easy book to read yet understand. Though it's the being introduced to undergraduate students.

After a while, I met a guy who works for Facebook. I told him about my plan and he said:

If you can understand this book, come and teach me!

He advised me to read The algorithm design manual instead.

While reading the Red book, as I call it, I started to implement Data structures by following this playlist. Data Structures Easy to Advanced Course - Full Tutorial from a Google Engineer . As you can imagine, I was complicating the process for myself, but I realised that after a long time of study, stop restart from the beginning process.

The turning point

A friend shared with me the Interviews.school, which should be a complete interview preparation guide. I found it's good, and decided to follow it. The plan was, follow the interviews school guide to have an overview about every type of problem that you can meet in an interview. And it worked!

Interviews.school wasn't enough for me, but it helped me to have a good idea about what to focus on!

The plan

Bear in mind developing problem-solving skills is like learning to play a musical instrument. Books and teachers can point you in the right direction, but only your hard work will take you there.

Start by learning about basic concepts
  1. Big O Time & Space
  2. Bit Manipulation
  3. Memory (Stack vs. heap)
  4. Recursion
  5. Dynamic Programming
Learning basic DS. In two simple to say, harder todo steps: Learn one by one, solve 10 problems on Leet-code.
  1. Dynamic arrays
  2. Array lists
  3. Linked Lists
  4. Stacks
  5. Queues
  6. Hash tables
  7. Trees
  8. Tries
  9. Graphs
  10. Heaps
  11. Vectors
Learn about basic algorithms
  1. Breadth-First Search
  2. Depth-First Search
  3. Binary Search
  4. Merge Sort
  5. Quick Sort
  • Solve the Cracking the coding interview book problems.
  • Solve the daily challenge on Leet-code
  • Read Leet-code discussions.
